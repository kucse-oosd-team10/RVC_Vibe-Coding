# Requirements Analysis Skill

## Purpose

Use this skill when analyzing, refining, organizing, or documenting project requirements.

The objective of this stage is to produce clear, testable, traceable requirements before design or implementation begins.

---

## Responsibilities

During this stage:

- Analyze functional requirements
- Identify non-functional requirements
- Clarify assumptions and constraints
- Identify out-of-scope items
- Detect ambiguity or inconsistency
- Refine unclear requirements
- Organize requirements systematically
- Prepare requirement traceability

Do not perform implementation unless explicitly requested by the user.

---

## Requirement Rules

- Every functional requirement should have a unique requirement ID whenever practical.
- Requirements should be testable.
- Requirements should avoid ambiguous wording.
- Requirements should describe behavior rather than implementation details.
- Out-of-scope items should be documented explicitly.

---

## Recommended Requirement Format

Example:

- R1: The RVC shall move forward while cleaning.
- R2: The RVC shall stop and turn when an obstacle is detected.
- R3: The RVC shall increase suction power when dust is detected.

---

## Expected Outputs

Possible outputs include:

- Requirement specification document
- Requirement list
- Requirement IDs
- Scope definition
- Assumption list
- Constraint list
- Requirement traceability table

---

## Verification Checklist

Before completing this stage:

- Are all requirements understandable?
- Are ambiguities identified?
- Are requirement IDs assigned?
- Are requirements testable?
- Are assumptions documented?
- Is scope clearly defined?

---

## Communication Rules

When reporting results:

- Clearly separate confirmed requirements from assumptions.
- Clearly identify unclear or missing requirements.
- Do not propose implementation details unless requested.
